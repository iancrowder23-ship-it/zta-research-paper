## Checklist

### A. The Core Idea in Plain Language (1 paragraph)
- [ ] "Badge scan on every door" analogy — even after entering the building, each room requires re-verification
- [ ] Key statement: trust is earned per-request, not granted at the door
- [ ] No jargon here — this should be readable by anyone

### B. Origin and Formalization (1 paragraph)
- [ ] Kindervag at Forrester, 2010 — where the term and idea came from
- [ ] Google BeyondCorp — first large-scale real-world implementation (post-2009 Operation Aurora)
- [ ] NIST SP 800-207 (2020) — formal standardization
- [ ] Executive Order 14028 (2021) — federal mandate (signals this is now mainstream, not fringe)

### C. The Three Foundational Principles (can be a short list or brief paragraphs)
- [ ] **Never Trust, Always Verify** — every request authenticated regardless of origin
- [ ] **Least-Privilege Access** — only the permissions needed for the task
- [ ] **Assume Breach** — design for failure, not just prevention

### D. What ZTA Is NOT (1 short paragraph)
- [ ] Not a product you buy
- [ ] Not a one-time project
- [ ] Not a complete solution on its own
- [ ] It is a philosophy + architectural strategy implemented incrementally
